1.e4 e6 2.d4 d5 3.Nc3 Bb4 4.e5 c5 5.a3 Bxc3+ 6.bxc3 Qa5

A risky but interesting idea, which is gaining popularity in modern practice.

7.Bd2 Qa4 8.Qb1

This is White's most popular move, but I am not sure that it is stronger than 8.Qg4 or 8.Nf3 . The idea is to prevent Black's strategic plan of ...b6 and ...Ba6 by threatening 9.Bb5+ .

8...c4

Black blocks the centre in order to play a closed game. The three bishops on the board are temporarily out of play. Black can also try 8...a6 9.Qb3 ( 9.Nf3!? ) 9...Qxb3 10.cxb3 +/=; on the other hand, 8...Nc6 9.Bb5 Qa5 10.Nf3 seems favourable for White.
