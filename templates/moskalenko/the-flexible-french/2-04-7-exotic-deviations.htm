GAME 34
Michael Stean - Jonathan Mestel
Marbella zt-B 1982 (3)

1.e4 e6 2.d4 d5 3.Nd2 c5 4.exd5 Qxd5 5.Ngf3 cxd4 6.Bc4 Qd6 7.O-O Nc6 8.Nb3 Nf6 9.Nbxd4 Nxd4 10.Nxd4 Be7!?

This possibility is not very popular (only 40 games in the MegaBase), but it can be useful for players looking for a quick draw, just as in the Rubinstein Variation with 3...dxe4 .

Instead, 10...Bd7 would be more ambitious, or even 10...a6 - see Games 35 and 36.

11.b3!?

With the idea of defending the bishop on c4 and preparing Bb2. There are three alternatives that should be checked:

(A) 11.Nb5!? The most aggressive move, looking for some tricks. A typical manoeuvre in this line, aiming to prepare a set-up with ...Bd6, controlling the b8-h2 diagonal.

(A) 11...Be7!? is a solid choice, after which some games finished after just a few more moves: 12.Re1 O-O ( 12...Qc7 13.Bb3 O-O 14.Bg5 Nd5 15.Bxe7 Nxe7 16.Qh5 Rad8 1/2-1/2 Balashov-Spassky, Munich 1979) 13.Bb3 Bc6 14.Bg5 1/2-1/2 Adams-Seirawan, Bermuda m-5 2000;

GAME 36
Michael Adams - Liviu-Dieter Nisipeanu
Sofia 2007 (3)

This brilliant game is the last one in our study of the ...Qxd5 variation and also the last game in Chapter 4.

1.e4 e6 2.d4 d5 3.Nd2 c5 4.exd5 Qxd5 5.Ngf3 cxd4 6.Bc4 Qd6 7.O-O Nf6 8.Nb3 Nc6 9.Nbxd4 Nxd4 10.Nxd4 a6

The real idea behind this move is hard to understand (is it aggressive or defensive?) , but it is the most popular move in practice. It forestalls any tricks on b5 and prepares the Sicilian-style advance ...b7-b5. However, according to general rules, White should be able to take advantage of his lead in development and he must attack at any price.

11.Re1!?

A forceful manoeuvre which obliges Black to abandon his original plans and to take good care of his e6-square.

(A) 11.Bb3 is an unnecessary manoeuvre, but the following game is a very good example: 11...Qc7 12.Qf3 Bd6 and now:

(A1) 13.h3 O-O 14.Bg5 Nd7 ∞ is the main line: 15.c3 b5 16.Bc2 Bb7 17.Qh5 g6 18.Qh4 Rfe8 19.Rfe1 Nb6 20.Re2 Be7 = and the position is equal, Tiviakov-Lalic, Port Erin 2005;

Statistics/Summary of the 'Hybrid Line' 3...c5 4.exd5 Qxd5

Total MegaBase: 4700 games, White wins=1685=56%, Black=1140=44%, Draw=1875.

At the moment, this variation is the most popular of the exotic deviations after 3.Nd2. But it is also remarkable that lately there have been no wildly courageous heroes in these lines. We see only terminators and their victims.

Dogmas and Weapons

After studying this chapter of weapons versus 3.Nd2, you can use the following repertoire with the black pieces:

* against Vasily Ivanchuk, respond with the authentic 3...Nf6.

* against Michael Adams, revert to tactics with 3...Be7.

* and finally, against Tarrasch, use the wild ideas of the Scandinavian-style 3...c5 4.exd5 Qxd5.

Good luck with your combats!

Postscriptum-Opinion about 3.Nd2

Statistically speaking, during the last 10 years most of the world's best players (Anand, Svidler, Shirov et al.) have hardly ever placed their knight on the d2-square. They prefer developing it at c3. This may be the most accurate move, and we will study it in the next two Parts.
