Conclusions regarding 'Fianchetto vs. Symmetry' â€” 7.Ngf3 g6 :
It is clear that the best option for White is 8.h4! . However, after the semi-blockading move 8...h6!? ( 8...Be7 is also interesting, whereas the immediate 8...Qb6 is risky), the h-pawn cannot progress and it becomes quite weak. White must revert to central action â€” his d4 pawn is in trouble - and solve the problem of his queenside development. In the attack against the centre Black can combine three important resources: ...f7-f6, ...Qb6 and ...g6-g5, at any moment during the opening. The positions arising are very complicated and neither side is guaranteed success. Michael Adams, Liviu-Dieter Nisipeanu and other grandmasters won several games with white, but later Mikhail Gurevich, Evgeny Bareev and the author of this book, among others, improved the line and its statistics. Since it is hard to imagine a thematic tournament where all doubts will be resolved, we will have to wait for new games in this line!

In the following game I was not able to improve my score against Ivanchuk, but thanks to it I learned a great deal about the French Defence, and this knowledge was very useful to me in subsequent games. What I especially learned about was the kind of positions Black should avoid.
