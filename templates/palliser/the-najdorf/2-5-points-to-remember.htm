Points to Remember

1) 7 Nf3 should not be underestimated. It leads to a tough positional battle and Black must make sure not to allow any early mishaps on the d5 and e6 squares.

2) The English Attack is a fashionable and dangerous weapon which Black must be prepared for. There is a fair amount of theory, but there are also general strategies and principles to help both sides through the more theoretical lines.

3) Black's play after either an early ...h5 or in the ...Be6 and ...Nbd7 lines is fairly thematic, and these are generally easier to play than the ...Be7 positions.

4) The main line of ...Be7 with 10...Nbd7 is popular amongst grandmasters, but is rather complex. At lower levels Black, if determined to gain a queenside attack, should still be successful with 10...a5!? and 10...b5!?, and neither is yet fully refuted from a theoretical perspective.
