Points to Remember

1) White's b3-bishop must be closely monitored and Black must be careful not to allow an early and strong sacrifice on e6.

2) 6...e6 blunts White's light-squared bishop. Should White reopen the a2-g8 diagonal with f4-f5, Black must be ready to seize the initiative and the d5-square with a key exchange sacrifice on c3.

3) 6.Bc4 e6 7.Bb3 permits Black a pretty reasonable choice between immediately fighting for the initiative with the ambitious 7...Nbd7!? and first preferring to neutralize White's pressure with 7...b5 8.0-0 Be7 9.Qf3 Qc7.

4) Black is in good shape against 6.Bc4, but he must be prepared for the position to become rather complex, even when neutralizing White's attacking intentions is his aim.
