Points to Remember

1) After 6.Be3 Ng4 7.Bg5 h6 8.Bh4 g5 9.Bg3 Bg7, Black must continue to play actively, especially by aiming to exploit his powerful g7-bishop and control of e5.

2) White must either counter 6.Be3 Ng4 aggressively or positionally by aiming to restrain Black's activity.

3) 10.Be2 should be met by 10...h5!, although both 10.Qd2 and 10.Be2 are far from played out and may well make a comeback when 10.h3 falls out of fashion.

4) 6.f3 Qb6!? appears to be a good companion to 6.Be3 Ng4, although Black must always remember to prioritize early queenside activity.
