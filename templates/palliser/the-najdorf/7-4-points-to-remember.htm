Points to Remember

1) Always respect the opponent's choice of variation and try to understand their aims. All the lines in this chapter have caught out several unprepared players over the years.

2) 6...e5 and 12...Bg4!-h5-g6 is an excellent way to neutralize White's attacking ideals after 6.f4.

3) Developing the queenside first gives Black useful extra flexibility against 6.g3.

4) Against the likes of 6.a4, but especially 6.h3, Black should remember that he may prefer something other than 6...e5.
