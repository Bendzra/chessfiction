Points to Remember

1) Gallagher's favourite ...Nc5 idea has ensured that former adherents of the Karpov system have turned their attention elsewhere in a bid for a positional edge.

2) Black should also try to follow up an exchange of knights on d5 with ...Nc5. White can then gain a passed d-pawn, but must face a mobile black kingside majority.

3) With no other minor pieces left, an unopposed knight on d5 against a black dark-squared bishop usually spells trouble. Black should always try to ensure that White must recapture on d5 with a pawn, but if White does win d5 for a piece, such as in the 8.Bg5 lines, Black must seek active counterplay on the flanks.

4) Meeting 9.a4 Be6 10.f4 with 10...exf4 should ensure Black of an easy game so long as he remembers to activate his e7-bishop with ...Ne8.

5) The popular 9.Kh1 should be treated with respect, but the equally crafty 9...b6 gives Black a perfectly acceptable position.
