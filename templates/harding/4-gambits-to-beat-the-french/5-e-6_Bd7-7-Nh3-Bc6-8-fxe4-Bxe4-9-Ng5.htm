6...Bd7

We have already seen an interesting example of this move, recommended by the German player Volker Hergert, in the Gibney-Conroy game.

7.Nh3!

As against 6...Nd7 , this appears to be best. The kingside is the focus of activity in this line. Instead 7.Rb1 is too slow because after 7...Bc6 the b-file is effectively closed: 8.Bb5 Qd7 (although 9.Qe2 Nf6 10.fxe4!? was suggested here by J.Tonjes of Bremen in Gambit Revue 2/1996) or 8.Qe2?! 9...Bg6

The most solid line for Black.

10.Rb1!

Alternatives analysed by Gutman:

a) 10.Qf3 Qd5 11.Qg3 h6 ;

b) 10.Bd3 Qd5 (not 10...h6? 11.Nxe6 Qe7 12.Qe2! ) 11.O-O Nf6 12.Rb1 Nbd7 13.Rb5 c5 with advantage to Black. Another possibility is 10...Nf6 11.O-O (Leisebein-Hergert, Gambit Revue 1/97 p17) 11...Nbd7 (Hergert).

10...b6

10...Nf6 is controversial: 11.Rxb7 O-O 12.Bd3 Qd5 13.Qf3 Bxd3 (After Hergert's original idea 13...Qxf3 14.Nxf3 Bxd3 15.cxd3 Na6 , White has 16.Rb5! ) 14.cxd3 Na6 15.O-O Qxf3 ( 15...Rfb8?! 16.Rxb8+ Rxb8 17.Ne4 Kh8 18.Bh6 Hergert) 16.Nxf3 Rfb8 17.Rxb8+ Rxb8 (Hergert) 18.Ne5 with a favorable endgame for White, claimed Gutman. In Gambit Revue 1/98, Hergert hit back with the continuation 18...Rb3 19.c4 Nb8 20.a4 Nbd7 21.Nxd7 Nxd7 22.Rd1 c6 and once more claimed that Black is a bit better in this line.

11.Bd3 Nf6 12.O-O

Gutman observes that, as in the variation 6...b6 , White has the extra tempo Rb1 which means Black doesn't have time for... Qd5.

12...O-O

Hergert's latest idea. Alternatives were analysed by Gutman:

a) 12...Qd5 13.Rb5 Qc6 14.Nf3!? Qxc3 15.Bb2 Qc6 16.Ne5 Qb7 17.Rb3 O-O 18.Qe1 Bxd3 19.Rxd3 Nbd7 20.Rh3 with the initiative;

b) 12...Bxd3 13.Qxd3!? Nbd7 14.Bf4 also with the initiative to White;

c) 12...Nbd7 13.Bf4!? Bxd3 (If 13...O-O then 14.Bxg6 hxg6 15.Qe1 ) 14.Qxd3 and Gutman prefers White.

After 12...O-O Hergert again claims some advantage for Black following either 13.Bxg6 hxg6 14.Qe1 Qd5 15.Qh4 Nbd7 or 13.Bf4 Nd5 . Therefore he disagrees with GM Gutman and claims that his 6...Bd7 is still good for Black if followed up this way.
