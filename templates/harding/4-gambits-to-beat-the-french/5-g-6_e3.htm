6...e3!?

Black returns the pawn to cause White some difficulties with kingside development and keep the f-file closed. However, this shouldn't really work as Black lacks a positive plan and White has a secure center.

7.Bxe3 Nf6 8.Bd3 b6

9.Ne2

9.Qe2!? also comes into consideration.

Bangiev has tried 9.Nh3 , e.g., 9...Ba6 10.O-O Bxd3 11.Qxd3 O-O 12.Bg5 Nbd7 13.Rfe1 Qc8 14.Nf2 h6 15.Bh4 Qb7 16.Ne4 Nd5 17.c4 Nf4 18.Qe3 Ng6 19.Bf2 e5 20.Qc3 Rae8 21.Ng3 f5 22.dxe5 Ndxe5 23.Bd4 with some advantage to White (Bangiev-Westphal, Celle 1994).

9...O-O 10.O-O Ba6 11.c4?!

Safer is 11.Bg5 Bxd3 12.Qxd3 Nbd7 (= Konikowski) while 11.Bxa6!? Nxa6 12.Bg5 has also been suggested.
